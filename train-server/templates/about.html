About This Service

The Problem

Traditional journey planners answer: "How do I get from A to B?"

But what if you're already on a train? Existing tools don't help you answer: "I'm on this specific service; what are my best onward connections?"

The Continuing Journey Planner fills this gap

How It Works

  1. Find your train by entering the next station and terminus (which your train will display)
  2. Enter your destination and we'll find the best onward connections

We use real-time data from the Darwin Live Departure Boards API, so journey suggestions account for current delays and predicted arrival times.

Features

  • Real-time departure information
  • Multi-leg journey planning
  • Walking connections between nearby stations (e.g., London termini)
  • Smart ranking of journey options by arrival time and changes

Limitations

  • Service IDs are ephemeral; you cannot bookmark specific services
  • No fare information — check with your ticket provider for validity

Technical Notes

Built with Rust, using data from the Rail Data Marketplace. This is an independent project and is not affiliated with National Rail, Network Rail, or any train operating company.

Design inspired by the bold, optimistic travel posters of 1930s–50s British Railways.
